Our Technology

At the core of PlasmaZen lies the biomimicry of nature's most dynamic force — lightning! By artificially generating millions of mini-lightnings every second, we unlock a world of transformative applications. How do we do it?

What is Plasma?

Plasma is an ionized state of matter composed of free electrons and ions. Interestingly, 99% of the known matter in the universe is in the plasma state. At PlasmaZen, we focus on a specialized form of plasma known as non-thermal or cold plasma.

How is Cold Plasma different?

Cold plasma is unique because it allows the heavier particles, like neutral atoms and ions, to remain at or near room temperature, while the electrons are energized to high temperatures between 10,000°C and 100,000°C. This temperature disparity lets us harness the reactive properties of plasma without the damaging thermal effects associated with high-temperature plasmas.

Generating Cold Plasma

To generate cold plasma, we apply extremely high voltages—over 20,000 volts—across very small gaps. A non-conductive dielectric prevents the plasma discharge from transitioning into an arc, ensuring a stable and controlled environment. The intense electric field strips electrons from atoms and breaks apart molecules, resulting in a dynamic mixture of charged particles and fragments.

The Reactive Symphony of RONS

When cold plasma discharges in the presence of air, it generates a variety of Reactive Oxygen and Nitrogen Species (RONS). These include hydroxyl radicals (OH*), superoxide anions (O2−), atomic oxygen (O*), nitric oxides (NOx*), alkoxyl radicals (RO*), and peroxynitrite (ONOO⁻). These reactive species are critical to the antimicrobial and sterilizing properties of plasma technology.

Microbial Inactivation Mechanism

These reactive species neutralize microbes by inducing oxidative stress on cell membranes, leading to lipid peroxidation and eventual cell lysis. They also disrupt proteins and DNA, inhibiting essential metabolic processes and contributing to microbial death. Importantly, these species have short lifespans and do not leave any residue on the substrate.
